01 — Executive Summary

What we're building and why now

Delivery platforms lose hundreds of millions of dollars annually to inefficient dispatch decisions — orders routed to the wrong courier, demand surges handled reactively, SLA breaches noticed only after they happen. The current dispatch workflow relies on static rule sets that cannot adapt to real-time demand signals, and on dispatcher judgment under high-pressure, high-velocity conditions.

SmartDispatch AI is a predictive intelligence layer embedded inside the existing dispatch workflow. It surfaces three capabilities dispatchers currently have to infer manually: where demand will spike in the next 30 minutes, which open orders carry the highest SLA breach risk right now, and which couriers are best positioned to absorb rebalancing. It does not replace dispatcher judgment — it makes that judgment faster and more accurate.

This PRD covers Phase 1 scope: the predictive demand heatmap, AI dispatch queue prioritization, and SLA breach early warning. Phase 2 (courier capacity optimizer) and Phase 3 (surge response automation) are defined at the roadmap level only.

02 — Problem Statement

The problem we're solving

Market context

The global last-mile delivery market exceeded $108 billion in 2023 and is growing at approximately 7.5% annually, driven by continued e-commerce growth and on-demand consumer expectations. At the same time, operational margins for delivery platforms are under sustained pressure. The average late delivery rate across major platforms sits between 12–18% depending on market, and each late delivery carries both a direct cost (re-delivery, compensation) and a long-term cost (customer churn, which studies put at 2–3x the cost of acquisition).

Core problem areas

Problem 1: Static dispatch rules cannot adapt to demand surge. Peak-hour coverage gaps of ~23% are structural, not incidental — they reflect a system that cannot predict demand 30 minutes ahead.

Problem 2: SLA breach visibility is entirely reactive. Dispatchers learn about breaches from customer complaints or end-of-shift reports — never from leading indicators before a breach occurs.

Problem 3: Courier capacity matching is manual at peak. During high-volume windows, dispatchers make 40–60 routing decisions per hour. Without ranked prioritization, high-value or time-critical orders are not systematically surfaced first.

04 — Jobs to be Done

What users are trying to accomplish

These JTBD statements were derived from synthesis of 12 dispatcher and operations interviews. They describe the underlying outcome users want, not the feature they want to use.

Ops Manager

"When I see demand spiking in the heatmap, I need to know which specific couriers to redeploy and to where — before SLAs start breaching — so I can act on a recommendation rather than improvise under pressure."

Dispatcher

"When I'm dispatching at peak hours and have 80 open orders, I need the system to pre-rank orders by urgency so I can triage in seconds rather than minutes, and not miss the orders that matter most."

Dispatcher

"When an order's SLA is about to breach, I need to see a clear warning before it happens so I can reassign to a faster courier rather than explaining a failure to a customer after the fact."

Courier

"When I'm being reassigned mid-route during a surge, I need to understand why and for how long, so the change feels fair and I can plan my next stop accordingly."

Phase 1 feature details

F-01  ·  AI Dispatch Queue Prioritization Phase 1

Machine learning model ranks all open orders in real time by composite urgency score. Dispatchers see a sorted queue rather than a raw list. Override capability preserved.

Time-to-SLA breach, customer tier, order value, nearest available courier distance, historical delay probability for zone + time-of-day.

Queue re-ranks within 30 seconds of new order events. Urgency score visible on each order card. Dispatcher override logged for model retraining.

15% reduction in late deliveries in pilot zone within 60 days of Phase 1 launch.

F-02  ·  Predictive Demand Heatmap Phase 1

Map overlay showing predicted order volume by zone for the next 30 and 60 minutes. Refreshes every 5 minutes using historical + real-time signals.

Historical demand patterns by zone/hour/day, current active order density, event calendar (sports, concerts), weather API, promotional event feed.

Demand prediction accuracy ≥70% within 30-minute window at zone level. Ops manager can filter by zone, time window, and order type. Available on web + mobile.

Surge coverage gap reduced from 23% to under 15% within 90 days of launch across pilot markets.

F-03  ·  SLA Breach Early Warning System Phase 1

Automated alert system flags orders predicted to breach SLA more than 15 minutes before breach occurs. Alert surfaces in dispatch queue and ops manager dashboard.

Current courier ETA vs. SLA window, traffic conditions, courier historical on-time rate for zone, order pick-up confirmation status.

Warning appears 15+ minutes before predicted breach. One-click reassignment action available from alert. False positive rate below 20% (validated against 30-day pilot data).

50% of at-risk orders successfully reassigned before breach within 60 days of Phase 1 launch.

06 — User Stories

Selected stories with acceptance criteria

US-001 Must have
As a dispatcher, I want to see all open orders ranked by urgency score so that I can prioritize the orders most at risk of SLA breach without manually reviewing each one.
  • Given I am on the dispatch screen, all open orders are sorted by urgency score (highest first) by default
  • Given an order's urgency changes, the queue re-sorts within 30 seconds without requiring a page refresh
  • Given I override the AI ranking, my action is confirmed and logged, and the override persists until I manually change it
US-002 Must have
As an operations manager, I want to receive an early warning alert when an order is predicted to breach SLA so that I can take corrective action before the breach occurs.
  • Given an order's predicted ETA exceeds the SLA window by 15+ minutes, an alert badge appears on the order card in my dashboard
  • Given I click the alert, I am shown the predicted breach time, current courier ETA, and a one-click option to reassign
  • Given I dismiss an alert without action, the system logs the dismissal and adjusts alert thresholds for future model retraining
US-003 Should have
As an operations manager, I want to see a real-time demand heatmap for the next 30 and 60 minutes so that I can pre-position couriers before demand spikes materialize.
  • Given I open the heatmap view, I see demand predictions overlaid on a city map by zone, color-coded by intensity
  • Given I select a zone, I see predicted order volume, current active couriers in zone, and a coverage gap indicator
  • Given the model confidence is below 60%, the zone displays a low-confidence indicator rather than a firm prediction
US-004 Nice to have
As a courier, I want to see a notification when I am being reassigned during a surge so that I understand why my route has changed and can plan accordingly.
  • Given a dispatcher reassigns me during a surge, I receive a push notification with the reason (surge area) and new order details
  • Given the reassignment extends my active time beyond my scheduled shift end, I am shown an estimated additional duration
07 — Success Metrics

Objective 1: Improve delivery SLA compliance in Phase 1 pilot markets
Reduce late delivery rate from ~15% baseline to 12.75% or below (15% relative improvement) Target: ≤12.75%
50% of flagged at-risk orders successfully reassigned before SLA breach within 60 days of launch Target: ≥50%
SLA early warning false positive rate below 20% (to preserve dispatcher trust) Target: ≤20%

Reduce average dispatch decision time per order from current baseline by 25% Target: -25%

09 — Risks & Dependencies

What could block or slow us down

RiskLevelMitigation
Model training data is insufficient in new markets with thin historical order data High Phase 1 restricted to 2 markets with 18+ months of clean historical data. New market onboarding requires 90-day data baseline before ML activation.
Dispatcher resistance to AI-ranked queues — override rate exceeds guardrail threshold High Co-design sprint with 6 dispatchers in weeks 3–4. Transparent scoring explainer on each order card. Override data feeds model improvement to increase trust over time.
Real-time data pipeline latency exceeds 30-second queue re-rank SLA under peak load Medium Load testing at 10,000 concurrent orders required before Phase 1 launch. Fallback to rule-based queue if ML inference latency degrades.
Courier equity concerns if AI routing consistently assigns longer routes to specific cohorts Medium Monthly bias audit on routing distribution by courier cohort, zone, and earnings impact. Algorithmic fairness review in Phase 2 scope.
Third-party weather and event API availability affects heatmap prediction quality Low Fallback model (historical patterns only) activates if external APIs are unavailable for more than 5 minutes. Model confidence score displayed to dispatchers in all cases.
10 — Out of Scope

What we are deliberately not building in Phase 1

Scope boundaries protect delivery focus and prevent feature creep. The following are explicitly out of scope for Phase 1:

Feature / CapabilityReason deferred
Fully autonomous dispatch (no human in loop) Dispatcher trust must be established in Phase 1 before autonomy is introduced. Phase 3 scope.
Integration with restaurant preparation time signals Requires partner API negotiation with 50+ restaurant chains. Phase 3 scope.
Cross-market or cross-city demand balancing Single-market model must be validated first. Phase 3 scope.
Courier earnings guarantee or incentive optimization Falls under compensation product ownership, not dispatch. Separate PRD required.
Customer-facing delivery tracking improvements Customer experience team owns that surface. SmartDispatch AI is an internal operations tool only.
